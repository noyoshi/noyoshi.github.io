Hi! My name is Noah Yoshida and I am a senior Computer Science major at the University of Notre Dame. I like working on projects that challenge me to learn new things, particularly in the realms of natural language processing, open source, Linux, and programming languages. I like thinking about how to create useful abstractions upon which you can easily write more powerful programs. I am currently learning a lot about both compilers and computer vision / NLP through my courses and research.

My current research focuses on how memes were used to influence voters in Indonesia's recent presidential election. I am trying to create a system that can extract semantic meanings from memes, whether that be the image data itself (image manipulation etc), or from the content of the image (text, people of interest, etc). I am aiming to publish a paper in Spring 2020 with a graduate student I am working with on this project.

I made this site to be small with no dependencies - while I do dabble in front end development, that is not something want to aim my career at. There are no font downloads and zero JavaScript imports. (This might change - I intend to have a slider for dark / light mode, and also a way to switch between Japanese and English, but I will do this all by hand with no imports or dependencies).
